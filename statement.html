Artist Statement

The first Inner Landscape appeared unexpectedly in 2015. While preparing the background of a painting, a landscape began to emerge — not from intention or imagination, not from a plan, but from something more instinctive. It felt as if the hand was acting on its own. Images surfaced slowly, unplanned, unexpected, yet fully formed. Since then, painting has become less a way of representing the visible world than a way of exploring what lies beneath it. Each work begins with an intuition rather than a narrative, allowing memories, emotions, and fragments of life to emerge gradually through the painting process. Rather than revealing everything at once, I enjoy leaving traces, subtle details, and quiet ambiguities that may be discovered over time. Built in layers, my paintings allow fragments of memory, imagined landscapes, and lived experience to quietly meet, unfolding slightly differently each time they are seen.
