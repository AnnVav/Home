Artist Statement

It all started in 2015, when the first “Inner Landscape” appeared.

I was quietly preparing the background for a new painting when, suddenly, a landscape began to emerge — not from imagination, nor from a plan, but from something more instinctive and internal.

It felt as if my hands were painting on their own, guided by an unknown energy. From deep within consciousness, images surfaced — unexpected, unplanned, and fully alive.

I love hiding things in my paintings. Sometimes this desire becomes so strong that I end up hiding an entire painting beneath another. It is an irrepressible impulse that imposes itself on me.

You may sometimes stand in front of two or three paintings at once without knowing it, as only the last layer is visible.

This is the magic of painting: we only show what we choose to show, while so much more remains hidden inside. A whole universe emerges from this inner world, revealed only through light.

Biography

Born in France in 1986, Annick Vavasseur is a contemporary painter whose artistic practice is rooted in both traditional craftsmanship and academic research.

She first studied English literature, art history, and trompe-l'œil techniques, including wood graining, faux marble, decorative finishes, and the recreation of panoramic wall paintings.

She later completed a Master's degree in Visual Arts Research at Panthéon-Sorbonne University.

Committed to continually refining her practice, she has attended numerous workshops in painting, watercolor, life drawing, drawing, and perspective at Paris Ateliers, the Ateliers des Beaux-Arts de la Ville de Paris, and the Louvre Museum.

Today, her work explores the relationship between memory, landscape, and imagination through a contemporary pictorial language.

“There is so much to learn in painting — it's a wonderful lifelong adventure.”
